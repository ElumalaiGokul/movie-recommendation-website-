Aval

  • Directed by: Milind Rau
  • Starring: Siddharth, Andrea Jeremiah, Anisha Angelina Victor, Atul Kulkarni
  • Music by: Girishh Gopalakrishnan
  • Release date: 2017
  • Language: Tamil
  • Box office: Hit

Plot

Aval is a psychological horror thriller that revolves around a haunted house and a family trapped in its sinister past. Dr. Krishnakanth (Siddharth) and his wife Lakshmi (Andrea Jeremiah) live a peaceful life in the mountains until a new family moves in next door. Their teenage daughter, Jenny (Anisha Angelina Victor), begins to experience paranormal occurrences, unveiling dark secrets linked to the house's history. As unexplained events intensify, Krishnakanth confronts the malevolent spirit haunting Jenny, leading to a terrifying battle between the living and the dead. With gripping suspense and spine-chilling moments, "Aval" redefines the horror genre with its eerie narrative and psychological depth.

Cast

Siddharth delivers a powerful performance as Dr. Krishnakanth, seamlessly portraying his character’s courage and vulnerability. Andrea Jeremiah shines as Lakshmi, adding emotional weight to the story with her nuanced acting. Anisha Angelina Victor is exceptional as Jenny, effectively capturing the fear and innocence of a possessed teenager. Atul Kulkarni plays Paul, a skeptic father who gradually faces the terrifying reality. The cast’s convincing performances bring authenticity to the supernatural narrative.

Production

Directed by Milind Rau, "Aval" is inspired by true incidents and influenced by classic horror narratives. Co-written and co-produced by Siddharth, the film stands out for its realistic portrayal of horror without relying on clichés. The screenplay is meticulously crafted, maintaining an eerie atmosphere throughout the film. The production values, combined with effective storytelling, make "Aval" one of the finest horror thrillers in Indian cinema.

Filming

Set against the backdrop of a misty hill station, the movie beautifully captures the eerie ambiance with its haunting visuals. The cinematography by Shreyaas Krishna plays a significant role in enhancing the horror elements, using shadows and dim lighting to create suspense. The old mansion, with its vintage architecture, adds to the haunting atmosphere, making the narrative more immersive and terrifying.

Themes and Influences

"Aval" explores themes of fear, loss, and the supernatural, delving into the psychological impact of paranormal experiences. It combines elements of Indian folklore with modern horror, creating a unique narrative that resonates with audiences. The movie effectively explores the conflict between science and superstition, questioning the boundaries of reality and the unknown. Influenced by classic horror films, "Aval" maintains an intense and gripping atmosphere without resorting to excessive gore.

Music

Composed by Girishh Gopalakrishnan, the music and background score of "Aval" play a crucial role in building tension and enhancing the horror experience. The haunting melodies and eerie sound effects amplify the suspense, making the terrifying moments more impactful. The soundtrack blends seamlessly with the narrative, contributing to the film’s chilling and atmospheric feel.

Release

Released in 2017, "Aval" received critical acclaim for its intelligent storytelling, realistic horror elements, and strong performances. It was praised for breaking the conventional norms of Indian horror movies, offering a fresh and sophisticated take on the genre. The film was also released in Hindi as "The House Next Door" and in Telugu as "Gruham", reaching a wider audience. Its spine-chilling narrative and atmospheric horror made it a memorable film, earning a special place in modern Tamil cinema.
