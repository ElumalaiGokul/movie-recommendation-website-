ONAAYUM AATUKKUTTIYUM

  • Directed by: Mysskin
  • Starring: Mysskin, Sri, Shaji Chen, Adithya Menon
  • Music by: Ilaiyaraaja
  • Release date: September 27, 2013
  • Language: Tamil
  • Box office: Critically Acclaimed

Plot

Onaayum Aattukkuttiyum is a neo-noir thriller revolving around a medical student who saves a wounded man, unknowingly getting involved in a dangerous cat-and-mouse chase. The movie explores the moral dilemma between right and wrong, with a gripping narrative that keeps the audience engaged.

Cast

Mysskin plays the mysterious character known as the "Wolf," showcasing his intense and enigmatic presence. Sri delivers a compelling performance as Chandran, a medical student caught in a web of crime and morality. Shaji Chen and Adithya Menon play pivotal roles, enhancing the film's depth and emotional intensity.

Production

Directed by Mysskin, the movie is known for its dark, realistic tone and intelligent screenplay. It effectively uses minimal dialogue and relies heavily on visual storytelling, creating an immersive experience. The narrative's philosophical undertones add layers to the suspenseful plot.

Filming

Shot mostly at night in Chennai, the film uses strategic lighting and shadow play to create an atmospheric, noir feel. The cinematography enhances the tension and mystery, contributing to the movie's unique visual style.

Themes and Influences

The movie explores themes of morality, redemption, and the thin line between good and evil. It delves into human nature, exploring the consequences of choices made in dire situations. Influenced by noir cinema, it presents a thought-provoking narrative with philosophical depth.

Music

Composed by Ilaiyaraaja, the background score is haunting and elevates the suspense and emotional gravity of the narrative. The music plays a crucial role in enhancing the film's dark, philosophical tone.

Release and Reception

Released on September 27, 2013, Onaayum Aattukkuttiyum received widespread critical acclaim for its bold narrative style and powerful performances. It was praised for its thought-provoking storytelling and was hailed as one of Mysskin's best works.
